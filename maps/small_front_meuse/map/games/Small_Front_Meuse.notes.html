Small Front: Meuse Corridor

Eight rounds. The Germans have to reach the Meuse. The Americans have to stop them, and keep holding everything worth holding.

The shape of it

Six bands run east to west, narrow to wide to narrow. Four staging territories funnel into a three-territory forest wall, open out across the nine-territory plateau where armour can manoeuvre, then narrow again onto five Meuse crossings.

The forest wall has no lateral roads. Losheim Gap, Clervaux and Vianden do not connect to each other, so they are three separate axes of advance. Switching axis means going back through the German rear, which costs turns you do not have. Under radius-1 fog you are committing to an axis without knowing where the American reserve sits.

Each Meuse crossing has exactly one approach: Durbuy–Huy, Havelange–Andenne, Ciney–Namur, Wellin–Dinant, Beauraing–Givet. The river is five doors, not a front. The west bank connects along itself, so once you are across you can roll up the river.

Roads are a subset of the movement edges. A spearhead can outrun its supply, and two turns out of supply removes it.

Scoring

Scored at the end of round 8.

  • 1 point per objective held: St. Vith, Bastogne, Marche, Neufchateau, Namur, Dinant
  • 2 extra German points for a supplied German land unit on any Meuse crossing
  • 1 extra German point for supplied German occupation of Bastogne or Marche
  • 2 extra American points if no supplied German land unit holds any Meuse crossing
  • Ties favour the Americans

The opening position is Americans 8, Germans 0. Taking objectives alone will not close that: each one is worth 2 points of swing, while the pair of Meuse bonuses is worth 4. The Germans have to reach the river.
